01 — Overview

The Event

What we produced, what it generated, and why it matters.

What Happened

Kozo. 1965 Mustang. 500+ hours. One room that mattered.

On May 14, 2026, No. 9 Agency co-produced the VIP dinner and press preview for Kozo's "Permanent Impermanence" — a solo exhibition presented by CART Department at Free Parking, a 19th-century West Village carriage house at 16 Morton Street. The centerpiece: a 1965 Ford Mustang Coupe covered in 500+ hours of hand-painted and tattooed art, including a full reinterpretation of François Lemoyne's Apotheosis of Hercules from the Palace of Versailles.

The exhibition ran May 15–24. The dinner and VIP preview on May 14 set the cultural context for everything that followed — press coverage, brand partnerships, and relationships now extending into future programming.

The Dinner

Chef Rōze Traore. HexClad. A menu built for the room.

Chef Rōze Traore (@rozetraore) curated a multi-course menu in dialogue with the exhibition: Yellowfin Crudo with pickled daikon and nori dust / Branzino with saffron beurre blanc and black truffles / Poached pears with hibiscus and goat cheese. HexClad cookware appeared throughout service as a visual element — a brand activation that became an editorial moment.

Chef Roze accepted a below-market fee to participate. That is a direct result of the relationship No. 9 Agency built and the caliber of room we created. HexClad — a direct No. 9 network relationship — co-presented the dinner alongside Kozo. The dinner in its form doesn't exist without us.

How we started. What we built. Where he landed.

The Growth Story

Back to 1,000,129. Built by No. 9, not accident.

No. 9 Agency managed Kozo's social strategy throughout the lead-up to this event — two planned shoot days per month, one painting, one tattooing. Process-first content philosophy. Broadcast channel for community engagement. A systematic rebuild of Kozo's digital identity ahead of the biggest show of his career.

The Follower Trajectory

Venice dip → 1M recovery

Before the campaign: Kozo had been at 1M, experiencing normal IG fluctuation. During the Venice Biennale trip (May 6–10), Instagram's bot purge dropped him to the low 999K range. The event week — May 14 dinner, then three press placements in 72 hours — drove the recovery and pushed him back above 1M.

The 30-day analytics show a sustained views spike around May 3 (peak ~395K in a single day) as event content started generating attention ahead of the show, followed by a second wave during the press window.

Content Strategy Executed

Built for positioning, not volume

Two shoot days monthly — one painting session, one tattooing session. Process-first: show the hand, the needle, the mark. Broadcast channel for community. The goal was to make May 14 feel inevitable to anyone who'd been watching. When the room filled with Futura and Mickalene Thomas, the audience had already been prepared for it.
