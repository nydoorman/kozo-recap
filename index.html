02 — World Building

World Building

What happened in the room. Who was there. What it was worth.

What we do

World-building is the craft of producing the rooms an artist's vision deserves — venue, partners, dinner, guest list, press preview, the cultural context that ties them together. The work between the artist and the audience. It's what No. 9 Agency does.

What Happened

May 14, 2026. Free Parking. West Village.

No. 9 Agency produced the VIP dinner and press preview for Kozo's "Permanent Impermanence" — a solo exhibition presented by CART Department at Free Parking, a 19th-century West Village carriage house at 16 Morton Street. The centerpiece: a 1965 Ford Mustang Coupe covered in 500+ hours of hand-painted and tattooed art, including a full reinterpretation of François Lemoyne's Apotheosis of Hercules from the Palace of Versailles.

The exhibition ran May 15–24. The dinner and VIP preview set the cultural context for everything that followed — press coverage, brand partnerships, and relationships now extending into future programming.

"One monumental 1965 Ford Mustang, adorned with more than 500 hours' worth of hand-painted and tattooed art, greets visitors. The exhibition edifies, inspires, and builds synapses between art forms."

Surface Magazine — May 20, 2026

The Dinner

Chef Rōze Traore. HexClad. A menu built for the room.

Chef Rōze Traore (@rozetraore) curated a multi-course menu in dialogue with the exhibition: Yellowfin Crudo with pickled daikon and nori dust / Branzino with saffron beurre blanc and black truffles / Poached pears with hibiscus and goat cheese. HexClad cookware appeared throughout service as a visual element — a brand activation that became an editorial moment.

HexClad co-presented the dinner alongside Kozo — the partnership shaped the form the evening took.

The Room

These people don't show up unless it's real.

The guest list was architecture — built deliberately to shape the cultural positioning of the event so that the right people wanted to be there. Futura, Mickalene Thomas, and Eric Haze don't show up for just any dinner. Their presence is a cultural endorsement that cannot be manufactured.

Follower Trajectory

Venice purge → event recovery → 1,000,129.

Going into the Venice Biennale trip (May 6–10), Kozo was at approximately 1M followers. Instagram's bot purge during that window dropped him to the low 999K range — nearly 1,000 followers lost in days. The event week, followed by three editorial placements in 72 hours, drove the full recovery back above 1M.

The 30-day view chart shows a clear two-wave pattern: a sustained pre-event spike starting around May 3, peaking at approximately 395K views in a single day as anticipation content built momentum, then a second acceleration during the press window — Cool Hunting May 17, Whitewall May 19, Surface May 20 — pushing the account back past the milestone. Views during the event 14-day window alone: 1,720,996. That is the direct output of the room built on May 14.
