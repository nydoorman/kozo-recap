Section 01

The Event

What we produced, and what it produced.

What Happened

On May 14, 2026, No. 9 Agency co-produced the VIP dinner and press preview for Kozo's "Permanent Impermanence" — a solo exhibition presented by CART Department at Free Parking, a 19th-century West Village carriage house. The centerpiece: a 1965 Ford Mustang Coupe covered in 500+ hours of hand-painted and tattooed art, including a full reinterpretation of François Lemoyne's Apotheosis of Hercules from the Palace of Versailles.

The exhibition ran May 15–24, 2026. The dinner and VIP preview on May 14 set the cultural context for everything that followed — press, social, and the brand relationships that are now extending into future programming.

The Venue

Free Parking, 16 Morton Street, West Village. CART Department's gallery — a 19th-century carriage house that has hosted cars-as-art programming with artists including Keith Haring and Ai Weiwei. Kozo's presentation is described by Whitewall as "one of the platform's most immersive exhibitions to date."

"One monumental 1965 Ford Mustang, adorned with more than 500 hours' worth of hand-painted and tattooed art, greets visitors. The exhibition edifies, inspires, and builds synapses between art forms."

Surface Magazine, May 20, 2026

The Dinner

Chef Rōze Traore — known for blending food with storytelling and immersive presentation — curated a multi-course menu that mirrored the exhibition's layered dialogue. HexClad cookware appeared throughout service as part of the event's visual language, turning a brand activation into an editorial moment.

Menu highlights included: Yellowfin Crudo with pickled daikon and nori dust / Branzino with saffron beurre blanc and black truffles / Poached pears with hibiscus and goat cheese.

No. 9 Agency brought Chef Roze to this project. Roze accepted a reduced fee to work with us because of the relationship No. 9 has cultivated. HexClad is a direct No. 9 network relationship — that dinner would not have existed in the form it did without us.

They Invested in the Experience

Not Just a Sponsorship

Golden Goose didn't buy a logo placement. They brought artisans. They designed custom tees with Kozo's figures on their star. They became part of the creative output of the show.

HexClad didn't just write a check for the dinner. Their cookware became a visual element of the dining experience. They co-presented the evening alongside Daniel Winer and Kozo.

DYNE's jewelry was displayed as sculpture alongside Kozo's work — not in a gift bag, not on a table. As art.

This level of integration is not available on any media plan. It requires trust, creative relationships, and someone who can translate brand desire into cultural participation. That is what No. 9 Agency does.

World Building, Not Brand Building

The value of this event is not what was sold. It is what was created: a world that now has gravity. A room that Futura and Mickalene Thomas chose to spend their evening in. A Mustang that will be written about for years. A chef who lowered her rate because the project was worth it.

Every brand in that room is now associated with an exhibition that landed in Surface, Whitewall, and Cool Hunting in the same week. That association doesn't expire when the show closes.

And every relationship No. 9 built to get those brands in that room — FlyBlack's interest in Basel, the Delano Hotel commission, the Golden Goose collab — that is the compounding return on what happened May 14.
